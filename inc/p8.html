Kelachandra Joseph George (born 24 August 1946) is a politician from the state of Karnataka, India, and the Minister for Bengaluru Development and Town Planning. He was previously the Home Minister of Karnataka. He was also the Minister of State (Independent Charge) Transport, Food and Civil Supplies in the Veerendra Patil government and Cabinet Minister for Housing & Urban Development in the S. Bangarappa government.

POLITICAL CAREER

George joined the Congress party in 1968. His political career started in 1969 when he was elected as the President of the Gonikoppal Town Youth Congress. During 1971–1972, He was the President of the Virajpet Taluk Youth Congress Committee.

From 1972 to 1973 he was the General of the Coorg District Youth Congress Committee and the President of this forum from 1973 to 1975. In 1975 and through 1978 he became the Treasurer of the Karnataka Pradesh Youth Congress Committee.

In 1982, He was the General Secretary of the All India Youth Congress Committee where he served till 1985. He later served as the General Secretary- Karnataka Pradesh Committee during and under the Presidentships of K. H Ranganath, N. Dharam Singh, S. M. Krishna, V. S. Kaujalgi, Allum Veerabhadrappa, Mallikarjun Kharge, Oscar Fernandes, Veerendra Patil and Janardhana Poojary.

In 1985, he was elected from the erstwhile Bharatinagar Assembly Constituency and became an MLA through 1994.

From 1985 to 1989, he was also the Secretary of the Congress Legislature Party. During the late 80’s and early 90’s, he was the Minister of State ( Independent Charge) Transport, Food and Civil Supplies under Chief Minister Veerendra Patil and Cabinet Minister for Housing and Urban Development under Chief Minister Bangarappa.

Currently, he is a State Election Commission Member, the General Secretary K.P.C.C, a Member of the A.I.C.C and Working Committee Member All India I.N.T.U.C. He is also having major stakes in Embassy Group.

George won the Sarvagnanagar Assembly constituency in Assembly Elections, 2013 by polling in 69,673 Votes Against His Nearest Rival Padmanabha Reddy of the Bharatiya Janata Party.

PROMISES

BENGALURU: With the Assembly Election just a few months away, Bengaluru Development Minister K J George on Thursday promised that all city roads will be smoother and better in the next four months. Bengaluru is not a planned city. Civic utilities were laid under the roads and this needs to be rectified, he said.

George, who attended a review meeting on ongoing projects in the city which was chaired by Chief Minister Siddaramaiah, later spoke to reporters and said, “Bengaluru has 1500 km of major roads of which 500 km will be asphalted afresh. Work orders have been issued to contractors who are expected to complete it by December. Asphalting will be done better than previous occasions and will be thicker too,’’ he said. Along with asphalting, footpath and drain, works will also be taken up.

George also said the white-topping of roads has been taken up on 100 km. “We want to make all major roads white-topped in the coming years. This apart, 20 km of roads will be developed under TenderSURE. All these works are expected to be completed in the next three to four months.”George also said the white-topping of roads has been taken up on 100 km. “We want to make all major roads white-topped in the coming years. This apart, 20 km of roads will be developed under TenderSURE. All these works are expected to be completed in the next three to four months.

Regarding high-density corridors like Old Madras Road, Bellary Road and Old airport Road, George said they will have better footpaths, raised medians, better streetlights, bus bays and signages for safety by December 10. After a request from IT giants, the government will develop 14 roads (80 km) that will connect Sarjapur Outer Ring Road at a cost of `500 crore of which `150 crore will be released as first instalment. The Hennur flyover will also be ready by December, he said.

The minister said the signal-free corridor from Old Airport Road to Hope Farm junction was delayed due to land acquisition issues. “We have sorted it out partly. While NAL and ISRO agreed to give 910 sqmt of land near Wind Tunnel Road, HAL too agreed to give 3,100 sqmt on Suranjan Das Road on the condition that we develop alternative road at a cost of `58 crore. Even at Kundalahalli we have acquired land. The only problem is at Hope Farm where private property owners are not agreeing to give up their land. If needed, we will issue land acquisition notices under emergency and we will pay them double the market price,he said.
