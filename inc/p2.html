    IMPROVE ACCOUNTABILITY : Krishna Byregowda wants ‘spot surgery’

    Bangalore: Agriculture minister Krishna Byregowda intends to conduct ‘spot surgery’ to bring in accountability among department officials. Spot surgery was a system adopted by his father C Byregowda as agriculture minister. He used to suspend officials on the spot following a complaint. “There are varied kinds of technology to monitor progress of the department. However, visiting the spot is needed some times,” Byregowda said on Tuesday after holding a review meeting with department officials. He said the government has an open mind on the issue of Genetically Modified (GM) crops and favors giving options to farmers to make informed choices. According to him, BT cotton, introduced in the state one-and-half decades ago, has benefited farmers to improve yields in an Eco-friendly manner. Batting for Bt, the new agriculture minister said people and government should keep their minds open. “We must give him (farmer) the options; let them choose”. Slamming the fertilizer and seed mafia for creating “artificial scarcity” of fertilizers, Byregowda said a special squad has been formed to crack down on elements who seek to create artificial shortage by hoarding.

    Road Development in Byatarayanapura

    Roads play an integral part in the day to day life of the people. Enhances the organization and well being of any constituency. Civil works were undertaken at an expenditure exceeding Rs. 190 crore to facilitate smooth flow of traffic in Byatarayanapura covering a total of 52 kilometers. Link Roads were built connecting Bellary National Highway (NH7) to the constituency. The roads which connect the urban and rural areas, development and widening of narrow roads, roads connecting the Bangalore International Airport road and other road development works have been successfully implemented.
